20,000 Leagues Under the Sea

Not 10,000 Leagues, as was said in an earlier episode, #86 The Life Aquatic. Also, an interesting note from that episode is that this "20,000 leagues" is not referring to a straight path downwards, perpendicular to the ocean's surface, but rather a horizontal journey, that happens to be under the sea. They recommend adding a comma after "Leagues" to your library copy, to make that distinction clear.

A league is about three miles, so the trip would be around 60,000 miles. Compare that to the deepest part of the ocean, Challenger Deep, in the Pacific's Mariana Trench, which is 6.9 miles.

The Caspian Sea/Lake/Ocean

Karen explains that the Caspian Sea is the largest lake, IF you consider it a lake. It's regularly called a sea, historically because of its saltiness. Since it is landlocked, it could actually be considered a lake. However, since oceanic plates are involved, it could be considered an small ocean as well! It just depends on the international law is.
